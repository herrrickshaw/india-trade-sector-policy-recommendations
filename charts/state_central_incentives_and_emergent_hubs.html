Beyond PLI: state incentives and emergent state hubs for the 5 FDI-priority sectors

Central PLI schemes are only half the incentive picture. This bulletin maps state-level incentive policies — capital subsidies, SGST reimbursement, land/power/stamp-duty concessions — layered on top of central schemes for the same 5 sectors as the FDI pitch deck, and identifies which specific states are emerging as recognized manufacturing hubs, with the evidence behind each claim.

SOURCES: ~130 web search/fetch operations across state government portals, official policy PDFs (Andhra Pradesh Electronics Manufacturing Policy 4.0 and Tamil Nadu Electronics Hardware Manufacturing Policy 2020 verified by direct primary-text extraction), PIB, and business press corroboration. Every claim below is sourced; unverifiable items are explicitly flagged rather than estimated. Retrieved 2026-07-18/19.

Errata & methodology notes
  • This bulletin doesn't re-verify every central PLI figure — those are already established in this repo's Sector & Policy Recommendations bulletin. This one is additive: state-level incentives and hub identification only.
  • Not every claim carries equal confidence. Two state policies (Andhra Pradesh Electronics Manufacturing Policy 4.0, Tamil Nadu Electronics Hardware Manufacturing Policy 2020) were verified by direct primary-PDF text extraction — the highest confidence tier. Most others rest on official government portals or PIB releases. A minority rest only on secondary/business-press corroboration because the primary PDF was image-based, blocked (HTTP 403), or unreadable — these are named explicitly in each sector's flags, not silently treated as equally solid.
  • "Emergent hub" here means evidence of real, named capital investment or an official cluster/park designation — not just the existence of an incentive policy. A state can have a well-funded incentive scheme with no landed investment yet (flagged per-sector where this applies), which is a different claim from being a hub today.
  • State policy terms change frequently and several are mid-transition as of this writing (e.g. Gujarat's EV policy lapsed June 2025 with no notified successor; Maharashtra's dedicated pharma/biotech policy was announced Feb 2026 but not yet published). Treat every specific percentage/cap here as a snapshot, not a permanent fact — always check the linked/named source for the current version before acting on it.
